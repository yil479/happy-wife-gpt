# Argument: In-law boundaries around the holidays

**Date:** 2026-01-05
**Trigger:** His mother invited us to a family trip and told us the dates before asking us.

## What happened
She'd already told other relatives we were "in" before either of us had agreed to go. I
wanted him to push back and say we needed to check our schedule first. He froze up and said
"I'll handle it" but then didn't say anything to her for two weeks, which left me feeling
like I was the only one willing to set a boundary with his family.

## How we felt
I felt like I was constantly cast as the "difficult" one for wanting boundaries, while he got
to stay the golden child. He felt caught in the middle, worried that saying no to his mother
would cause a bigger blowup than either of us wanted to deal with.

## How it was resolved
We agreed on a rule: whichever of us the request comes from is the one who responds to their
own family, even if it's uncomfortable — I don't call his mom, he doesn't call mine. He
practiced what he wanted to say out loud with me first, which made it easier to actually say
it. He called her, explained we needed to check dates before committing, and it went fine.

## Reflection
The anxiety about the conversation was worse than the conversation itself. What mattered most
to me wasn't the specific outcome — it was not being the only one carrying the boundary.
