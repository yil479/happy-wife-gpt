# Argument: Screen time rules for our daughter

**Date:** 2026-02-20
**Trigger:** He let our daughter watch tablet videos through dinner "just this once" for the
third time that week.

## What happened
I brought up that we'd agreed on no screens during meals, and he said he was just trying to
get through a stressful evening and didn't think one exception was a big deal. I pointed out
it wasn't actually "one exception" — it kept happening, and I felt like I was the only one
enforcing rules we'd both agreed to.

## How we felt
I felt like the "bad cop" by default, always the one saying no while he got to be the fun
parent. He felt judged for making a reasonable in-the-moment call under stress, and like I
was treating a minor thing as a character flaw.

## How it was resolved
We talked after she went to bed instead of in front of her. We agreed rules need to bend for
genuinely hard days (he'd had a rough one) but that "hard day" shouldn't quietly become the
new normal. We now text each other "screen exception today" so it's a visible, mutual
decision instead of a unilateral one that the other person discovers after the fact.

## Reflection
Consistency between us mattered more to our daughter than the rule itself. Making exceptions
visible instead of silent fixed the resentment without us having to be rigid.
