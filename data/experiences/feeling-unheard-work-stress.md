# Argument: Venting about work turned into a fight

**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Trigger:** I came home wanting to vent about my manager, and he jumped straight to solutions.

## What happened
I started telling him about a frustrating meeting, and within a minute he was listing things
I should do differently — "just email her and lay it out," "you should escalate this." I
snapped and said "I don't need you to fix it, I need you to listen," and he got defensive,
saying he was just trying to help and didn't understand why that was wrong.

## How we felt
I felt dismissed, like my feelings were a problem to be solved rather than something worth
sitting with. He felt like he couldn't win — that offering help was punished, and he didn't
know what "just listen" was supposed to look like in practice.

## How it was resolved
We came up with a simple signal: when one of us starts venting, we now say "solutions or just
listening?" up front. It sounds a little clinical but it works — it removes the guessing game
and stops him from feeling rejected for trying to help.

## Reflection
We both wanted the same thing (for me to feel better) but were using completely different
definitions of support. A five-second question at the start saves us the whole fight.
