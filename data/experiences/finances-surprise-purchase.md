# Argument: Unplanned purchase

**Date:** 2026-04-02
**Trigger:** A $900 charge on the shared credit card for a new road bike.

## What happened
I saw the charge before he'd mentioned it to me and immediately felt blindsided. We'd agreed
verbally a few months back to check in on anything over $200 that wasn't a bill. He said he'd
been meaning to bring it up but got busy, and that he felt like he was being treated like a
teenager who needed permission to spend his own paycheck.

## How we felt
I felt like the agreement we'd made didn't matter to him, and that made me anxious about
whether we're actually aligned on savings goals. He felt controlled and resentful, like his
autonomy was being questioned over a purchase he could clearly afford.

## How it was resolved
We separated two issues: the process (he should have said something before, not after) from
the amount (which honestly wasn't a big deal financially). We set up a shared note where big
purchases get flagged *before* the card is used, not confessed after. We also agreed each of
us gets a smaller "no questions asked" personal spending amount each month so this kind of
thing stops feeling like it needs a committee.

## Reflection
Money fights are rarely about the number. This one was about trust and autonomy pulling in
opposite directions, and a small process fix took the heat out of it.
